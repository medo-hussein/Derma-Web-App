Dry Skin

Dry skin generally produces less natural oils than other types of skin. This may cause it to appear dull and become rough, flaky, or even scaly. It often feels tight or less elastic, noticeably dehydrated, and may be prone to showing more visible fine lines. In addition, it may become itchy or irritated.

Oily Skin

Oily skin produces an excess of sebum that causes the skin to appear shiny and feel greasy especially throughout the T-zone (forehead,nose,and chin). People with oily skin be more prone to enlarged pores,acne blemishes,black and whiteheads.

Normal Skin

Normal skin is balanced feeling neither too dry nor too oily. It is not prone to breakouts, flakiness, or feeling greasy or tight.
